Size of Bitcoin Network

[?]

Size of Bitcoin Network refers to the number of people that are participating in the Bitcoin monetary system

The default network size of 7.8 Billion is based on close to 100% of the current world population being part of the Bitcoin network. (source)

On the Stephan Livera Podcast at 27:30, @Croesus_BTC estimates that roughly 2.2 billion people have $10k or more of wealth. You could consider this population to be the potential meaningful adopters of Bitcoin.

Current estimates for how many people own bitcoin varies wildly. Crypto.com released a report estimating that current global crypto users reached 106 million in January 2021 (source)

Bitcoin Supply

[?]

There is a cap of 21 million Bitcoins that will ever exist, but the last bitcoin will won't be mined until the year 2140.

There are currently just under 18.7 million Bitcoin is circulation today (source)

It is unknown exactly how many bitcoins have been lost forever due to lost private keys. Some argue that over 4 million bitcoin have already been lost, which would bring the current circulating supply to roughly 15 million ( source)
